§ 01 — Market Structure

Two markets, not one.

Most people lump "EdTech" together. In Nepal, there are really two parallel industries with almost no overlap in customers, sales motion, or pricing. Get this split right and the rest is easy.

§ 02 — Catalog A

The back-office seven.

These platforms run the institution itself — admissions, billing, exams, payroll, parent communication. Sold as multi-year licenses to schools and colleges; never directly to students.

Cat. A Administrative Platforms 07 providers
Veda
by Ingrails Pvt. Ltd.
Market Leader

The default choice. Used in 900+ schools and colleges nationwide. Pivoted hard during COVID by adding Zoom-integrated online classes, online admissions and digital fee collection — and kept that ground.

Best for
Mid-large private schools & colleges
Strength
Largest install base; mature parent app
Example
Adopted by reputed valley schools
e-School
by e-Zone International
Enterprise

The most institutional option in Nepal — and the only one with serious public-sector pedigree. Built TU's EMIS and the UGC's FMIS / EMIS systems under World Bank funding, plus 25+ other government projects.

Best for
Universities, regulators, top-tier colleges
Strength
Government / World Bank track record
Example
Tribhuvan University EMIS, UGC FMIS
Nimble Academic ERP
by Nimble Infosys
IRD-Certified

Wins on Nepal-specific finance. Billing and accounting modules are IRD-certified, and the system natively integrates eSewa, Khalti and ConnectIPS. Includes optional GPS-geofenced bus tracking.

Best for
+2 colleges, finance-heavy institutions
Strength
Tax-compliant accounting + payment gateways
Example
Engineering & medical +2 colleges

independent vendor

independent vendor
Modular

The pick-and-choose option. Pitches itself as an AI-driven cloud ERP where schools select only the modules they need — biometrics, SMS, online exams, payment gateway, mobile app — instead of paying for the whole stack.

§ 03 — Catalog B

The front-of-class seven.

These don't run schools — they teach. Some are licensed by institutions; others sell directly to students; a few are publicly-funded. Their unit economics differ wildly, and so does their reach.

Cat. B Learning Platforms 07 providers
Fuse Classroom
by Fusemachines
AI-Native

The most globally-styled learning platform built in Nepal. Fusemachines is a Kathmandu-rooted AI company with US presence; its classroom platform layers AI tools over standard LMS features for assignments, tests and grading.

Best for
Premium schools wanting AI features
Strength
Polished UX, AI-driven workflows
Example
International curriculum schools
mySecondTeacher
commercial subscription
NEB-Tuned

Hyper-focused on +2 / NEB students. Library of 4,000+ video lessons mapped lesson-by-lesson to the Curriculum Development Centre's syllabus. Often distributed through schools rather than pure D2C.

Best for
NEB Grade 11–12 students
Strength
Tight CDC syllabus alignment
Example
+2 students prepping for boards
Kullabs
by Kul Techno Lab
Free

Nepal's homegrown answer to Khan Academy. Free notes, videos, exercises and practice tests for grades 6–12, available via web and Android. Long-running and widely used by self-studying students.

Best for
Self-study, grades 6–12
Strength
Free + localised curriculum content
Example
Students supplementing classroom learning
Mero School
commercial D2C
Subscription

Direct-to-consumer video classes. Targets high-school and bachelor's level students with affordable subscription pricing — students sign up individually, not via their school.

Best for
Self-paced high school + undergrad
Strength
Affordable D2C model
Example
Bachelor's students self-studying
MeroSiksha
commercial / freemium
Test Prep

Niche but useful — past papers, model solutions, custom-practice quizzes and flashcards. Not a course delivery platform; more of a revision and exam-prep companion.

MoEST · Govt. of Nepal

The country's official e-learning portal, launched during COVID by the Ministry of Education with CEHRD. Curriculum content for grades 1–10, available bilingually in Nepali and English. Free to all.

§ 09 — Real-world Picks

Six common scenarios.

Same answer engine, applied to actual situations. If one of these is yours, you basically have your shortlist.

i.
A 1,200-student private school in Lalitpur upgrading from paper.

Mid-large institution, mainstream needs, parents expecting an app. The default safe choice — large install base means stable product and easy hiring of admin who already know the system.

→ VEDA · backup: NIMBLE
ii.
A constituent campus of a public university running its EMIS.

Regulatory complexity, integration with university-wide systems, government reporting. Only one vendor in Nepal has actually shipped this at scale.

→ E-SCHOOL (e-Zone)
iii.
A +2 college handling crores in annual fees.

Tax compliance is the make-or-break. Pick the system whose accounting module is IRD-certified out of the box and supports all three local payment gateways natively.

→ NIMBLE ACADEMIC
iv.
A small community school in a hill district, ₨ 50K/year IT budget.

Scale is wrong for Veda or e-School pricing. Either go budget-tier ERP or just use the free public tools and a Google account for communication.

→ PAATHSHALA · or OLE NEPAL + GOOGLE
v.
An IB / Cambridge school adding AI-driven assessment.

Premium parent base, international curriculum, expectations matched to global EdTech UX. The locally-built but globally-styled option fits.

→ FUSE CLASSROOM (+ a separate ERP)
vi.
A Class 12 student prepping for NEB boards on their own.

Doesn't need an institution at all. Wants video lessons mapped to the syllabus + plenty of past papers for revision. Two stacking subscriptions cover it.

→ MYSECONDTEACHER + MEROSIKSHA

Method & Caveats

This report compiles publicly available information from vendor websites, government project records, and Nepali tech publications. Where reach is shown, figures are directional inferences from public references — not audited counts.

Pricing is omitted because most vendors quote per-deployment rather than publishing rate cards. Request a demo before committing. The market shifts every few quarters; revisit before any procurement decision.
