§ 03 / How to Acquire First 20 Customers (Months 1–6)

Distribution matters more than product

Building is easy. Getting hotels to buy is hard. Here's the playbook that actually works in Nepal.

1. Direct Sales to Hotel Chains

Target managers at Hyatt Regency, Hilton, large independent chains. Offer white-label integration. One deal = 10–20 hotels. Cost: time + 1 trip to Kathmandu.

2. Hotel Consultant Partnerships

Partner with hospitality consultants, architecture firms, interior designers who work with hotels. They recommend your tool in their proposals. Revenue share: 20–30%.

3. Hotelogix / Cloudbeds Integration Marketplace

Get listed as plugin/integration. Hotelogix has 5,000+ hotels; Cloudbeds has 26,000+. Even 1% = 250+ hotels. Free listing, ~15% affiliate margin.

4. YouTube + SEO

Create "How to [problem]" videos in Nepali. Rank for "होटल बुकिंग सॉफ्टवेयर" (hotel booking software Nepali). Cost: content creation (in-house). ROI: inbound leads at 0 CAC.

5. Hotel Groups & Associations

Nepal has hotel associations (Kathmandu Valley, Pokhara, Chitwan hotel groups). Present at annual meetings. Group discounts: 10+ hotels = 20% off.

6. Reseller / Agency Model

Partner with local IT agencies, management consultants. They sell your tool, keep 30–40%, you keep 60–70%. Adds 5–10 customers per partner in month 2.

Why Cold Email / Ads Don't Work in Nepal

Hotel GMs don't check spam. Facebook/Google ads cost too much per click ($0.50–2). Trust = everything in Nepal. Build relationships first, sell second. Your first 20 customers will come from referrals, not ads.

§ 04 / Your 12-Month Roadmap

Month-by-month: from idea to ₹1 crore annual revenue

Months 1–2: Validate + Hire
Do: Interview 20 hotel managers. Validate problem (booking automation, staff training, energy waste). Hire 2 developers, 1 designer. Register company. Budget: ₹1–2 lakh (salaries) + ₹20K (office/servers).
Months 3–5: Build MVP + Beta Testing
Do: Build MVP (one service). Beta test with 3–5 early adopter hotels (offer free for 2 months in exchange for feedback). Refine. Budget: ₹3–5 lakh in salaries + servers.
Months 6–8: Launch + Distribute
Do: Official launch. Launch integration partnerships (Hotelogix marketplace). Approach hotel chains. First paid customers. Target: 20 customers. Budget: ₹4–5 lakh in salaries + ₹50K marketing/travel.
Months 9–10: Scale + Iterate
Do: Launch 2nd product (add to your portfolio). Grow first product to 30–40 customers. Hire account manager for customer support. Budget: ₹5–6 lakh in salaries.
Months 11–12: Optimize + Plan Growth
Do: Hit 50+ total customers across services. Revenue: ₹30–40 lakh. Margin: 60–70%. Plan hiring (4–5 developers by month 15). Prepare for ₹1 Cr target. Budget: ₹6–8 lakh in salaries.

Payoff Timeline

Month 3–4: First revenue. Month 5–6: Breakeven on MVP. Month 8: 30% monthly profit margin. Month 12: ₹30–40 lakh annual revenue on ₹15–20 lakh annual cost = ₹15–25 lakh net profit. Scale to ₹1 Cr in year 2–3 (hire team, add 3–4 more products).

§ 05 / How to Fund Your Startup

You don't need VC. Here are 4 realistic paths.

Path 1: Bootstrap (₹5–10 Lakh)

Personal savings + friends/family. Hire 2 developers (part-time or junior). Work from home office. Payoff: Month 5–6. Risk: High (your money). Timeline: 12–18 months to ₹50 lakh revenue.

Path 2: Service Revenue (₹0 upfront)

Do freelance/outsourcing work on Upwork (₹20–40K/month). Hire 1–2 juniors, build SaaS at nights/weekends. Use service revenue to fund product. Payoff: Slow but sustainable. Timeline: 18–24 months to ₹1 Cr.

Path 3: Angel Investors (₹10–20 Lakh)

Nepal angels, tech funds (Ndure, Kathmandu-based VCs), startup ecosystems. Need: business plan, first 5 customers (proof), team resumes. Equity: 10–20%. Timeline: 1–2 months to raise.

Path 4: Accelerator Programs (₹5–15 Lakh + mentoring)

StartUp Nepal, Kathmandu-based programs, NepSAC. Apply with idea + team. Get ₹10–20L + 3-month mentoring + pitch to investors. Equity: 5–10%. Timeline: 6-month program.

Most Realistic for Indies: Path 1 + Path 2 Hybrid

Bootstrap with ₹5 lakh (personal savings + friends). Do outsourcing work for ₹30K/month. Hire 1 junior developer (₹20K). Build SaaS in parallel. Hit profitability in month 6–8. Then raise ₹10L Series A to scale.

§ 06 / Your Next Steps (This Week)

Start here. Do this.

Day 1–2: Choose Your Service
Pick ONE from the 10. Most likely: Nepali Chatbot, Channel Manager, or WhatsApp Integration (fastest to payback). Call 10 hotel managers. Record their pain points. Write down 3 "must-haves" for your MVP.
Day 3–5: Validate Problem + Willingness to Pay
Ask each hotel: "Would you pay ₹8,000/month for [feature]?" Record "yes," "maybe," or "no." If 5+ say "yes," you have product-market fit. Go to step 3.
Day 6–7: Hire First Developer
Post on LinkedIn, Upwork, local Kathmandu groups. Look for mid-level developer (3–5 years exp, React/Node/Python). Budget: ₹30–50K/month. Negotiate 3-month contract (trial).
Week 2–4: Define MVP Spec
Write down exact features (in plain English, not technical jargon). Show to 3 hotels. Ask: "Does this solve your problem?" Iterate based on feedback. Finalize spec in week 4.
Week 5–8: Build MVP
Hire second developer if needed. Build MVP using pre-built tools/frameworks (don't reinvent). Use Next.js/React, Firebase/PostgreSQL, Stripe/Khalti for payments. Target: functional in 8 weeks.
Week 9–10: Soft Launch
Approach 3–5 early adopter hotels. Offer free for 2 months in exchange for feedback. Record all issues. Iterate weekly. Document everything.
Week 11–12: Official Launch + First Sales
Go live. Launch integration partnerships (Hotelogix, etc.). Close first 2–3 paid customers. Do press release (local news). Start YouTube channel (5–10 videos on "problem + solution").

By Week 12, You Should Have

MVP in production. 3–5 paying customers. ₹30–50K monthly recurring revenue (MRR). Clear product-market fit signals. Path to ₹1 Cr annual revenue visible. If you've hit this, scale. Hire more. Add products.
